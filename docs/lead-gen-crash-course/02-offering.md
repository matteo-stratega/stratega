# **Module 2: Structuring Your Offering**

**Video 1: Crafting a Value Proposition That Resonates**

**Key Concepts**:

1.	**Introduction to the Value Proposition**

•	Why it’s critical to craft an offering that speaks directly to your ICP’s needs, challenges, and goals.

•	The difference between features and benefits: people don’t buy products, they buy solutions.

2.	**Understanding the ICP’s Core Problem**

•	How to align your offering with the core challenges or “jobs to be done” that your ICP is trying to solve.

•	Example: If your target audience is marketing leaders in tech, their core problem might be improving ROI on campaigns. Your value proposition should center on how your product specifically solves that problem.

3.	**Benefits Over Features**

•	Discuss how to translate product features into real-world benefits that matter to your ICP.

•	Example: Instead of highlighting that your tool “automates reporting,” explain how it “saves 10 hours of manual work each week, allowing your team to focus on strategic decisions.”

4.	**Proof Points: Use Case Studies and Social Proof**

•	Using real examples and social proof, such as case studies or testimonials, to back up your value proposition.

•	Example: “Here’s how our platform helped a mid-sized software company grow their sales pipeline by 30% in six months.”

**Video 2: Personalization and Refining Your Offering**

**Key Concepts**:

1.	**Personalizing Your Offering**

•	How to adjust your value proposition based on the ICP’s stage in the client journey (Awareness, Consideration, Decision).

•	Example: In the Awareness stage, you might focus on educational content, while in the Decision stage, you’re offering concrete data and pricing models.

2.	**Iterating Your Offering Through Feedback**

•	Why you should constantly evolve your offering based on customer feedback and market changes.

•	Example: After validating your ICP, you find that enterprise clients need more customizable features, so you refine your product to match that demand.

3.	**Positioning for Different Segments**

•	How to tailor your messaging for different market segments or verticals within your ICP.

•	Example: In the SaaS industry, your offering might focus on growth and scalability, whereas in healthcare, you focus on compliance and security.

4.	**Finalizing Your Value Proposition for Scale**

•	How to ensure that your offering can be delivered at scale while still retaining a personalized touch.

•	Example: “Once you’ve tested your value proposition on a smaller batch of 200 leads, you’re ready to refine and scale that message to 1000+ prospects.”

**Real-Life Examples for Module 2**

“If you’re selling a project management tool to a tech startup, focus on benefits like ‘keeps your teams aligned’ rather than technical features like ‘supports Gantt charts.’ The goal is to make your offering feel like the solution to their specific challenges, such as managing remote teams or hitting deadlines faster.”

“When selling to enterprise-level customers, case studies become your best friend. For example, ‘We helped XYZ Corp save $50,000 per year by automating their internal processes,’ gives your prospect something tangible and credible to relate to.”

“A financial services company in the Awareness Stage might not yet know that compliance tools can save them money on penalties. “In the SaaS world, companies often look for scalability. Your offering should emphasize how your tool grows with them — ‘Our platform can handle 10 users or 10,000.’ Meanwhile, for healthcare, you might focus more on security: ‘Our platform meets all HIPAA compliance standards.’ Tailoring to the segment makes your offering much more compelling.”

# Video Script

**Introduction (20 seconds):**

“Welcome to Module 2! Now that we’ve defined and validated your ICP, it’s time to craft an offering that truly resonates. In this session, we’ll explore how to create a value proposition that speaks directly to the needs of your prospects. Remember, people don’t buy products; they buy solutions to their problems.”

**Section 1: What is a Value Proposition? (1 minute 10 seconds)**

“Your value proposition is the core of your offering. It’s the reason why a prospect should choose your solution over someone else’s. But the key here is that your value proposition should not focus on product features — it needs to focus on the benefits your product brings to your ICP.

Think of it this way: Your product might automate a process, but what does that mean for your ICP? Does it save time? Does it improve efficiency? These are the real-world benefits that matter to your prospects.”

*Example:*

“Let’s say you’re selling a customer support tool. Instead of focusing on features like ‘24/7 chat support,’ translate that into a benefit: ‘Our tool helps you reduce customer response times by 50%, increasing satisfaction and retention.’ This speaks directly to what the customer is trying to achieve.”

**Section 2: Aligning Your Offering with the ICP’s Core Problem (1 minute 10 seconds)**

“To make your value proposition effective, it needs to directly address the ICP’s core problem — their job to be done. Think about what keeps them up at night. What are their pain points, and how does your product solve those problems?

It’s important to personalize your offering to match the specific needs of your ICP. When you can demonstrate that your product solves a relevant problem, you shift from being just another tool to a must-have solution.”

*Example:*

“Imagine you’re targeting marketing leaders in tech companies. Their core problem might be proving ROI on campaigns. So instead of saying, ‘Our tool tracks campaign performance,’ you’d say, ‘Our tool helps you measure ROI more accurately, so you can optimize ad spend and improve campaign results by 30%.’ This message resonates because it directly ties your product to solving their specific challenge.”

**Section 3: Benefits Over Features (1 minute 10 seconds)**

“One of the biggest mistakes companies make is focusing too much on features. While it’s great that your product has a lot of functionalities, prospects care about how those features translate into tangible results for them.

Always ask yourself: What’s in it for them? How do the features of your product make their life easier, solve their pain, or create new opportunities?”

*Example:*

“Let’s take an email marketing platform. A feature might be ‘customizable templates,’ but the benefit is ‘reduce the time it takes to create email campaigns by half, allowing your marketing team to focus on strategy instead of execution.’ By focusing on time-saving and strategic benefits, you connect with what really matters to the prospect.”

**Section 4: Proof Points: Use Case Studies and Social Proof (1 minute 10 seconds)**

“Once you’ve crafted your value proposition, back it up with proof points. Use case studies, testimonials, or hard data to show that your solution works. Real-world examples give credibility to your offering, especially when they show measurable results.

People want to know that others in their industry or similar roles have succeeded with your product.”

*Example:*

“If you’re selling to enterprise-level customers, a case study could be something like, ‘We helped a Fortune 500 company automate their billing process, reducing costs by 25% within the first quarter.’ This provides a concrete, relatable example that adds weight to your offering.”

**Conclusion (40 seconds):**

“Your value proposition is more than just a pitch — it’s a promise that your product will solve a specific problem for your ICP. By focusing on the core benefits rather than features and supporting your claims with real-world proof points, you’ll create a compelling reason for your prospects to engage with you.

In the next video, we’ll cover how to personalize and refine your offering even further, ensuring that it resonates with different segments and at different stages of the client journey. See you there!”

**Video 2 Script: Personalization and Refining Your Offering**

**Introduction (20 seconds):**

“Welcome back! In this session, we’ll dive into how to personalize your offering for different segments of your ICP and refine it over time based on feedback. Remember, your value proposition should evolve just like your ICP. Let’s get started.”

**Section 1: Personalizing Your Offering by Journey Stage (1 minute 10 seconds)**

“Your prospects are all at different stages of the client journey — some are just becoming aware of their problem, while others are ready to make a decision. To make your value proposition resonate, you need to tailor it to each stage of the journey.

At the **Awareness Stage**, focus on education and raising awareness about the problem. At the **Consideration Stage**, show them why your solution is the best fit, and at the **Decision Stage**, offer them clear, actionable information like pricing and ROI.”

*Example:*

“If you’re targeting HR managers in the Awareness Stage, you could provide a blog post about the latest trends in employee engagement. In the Decision Stage, you’d offer a case study showing how your tool increased employee engagement by 20% in a similar-sized company. Tailoring the message helps you move them along the journey.”

**Section 2: Iterating Your Offering Based on Feedback (1 minute 20 seconds)**

“One of the most important things you can do is iterate your offering based on real-world feedback. This means listening to your customers, analyzing data, and refining your messaging and product accordingly.

If you notice that certain features are consistently overlooked or that prospects are asking for more customizable options, that’s a sign you need to adjust your value proposition or even improve your product.”

*Example:*

“Let’s say you launch a software tool and realize that mid-sized companies keep asking for more detailed reporting features. Based on this feedback, you can refine your product to meet that demand and adjust your value proposition to highlight your tool’s new advanced reporting capabilities. This responsiveness shows prospects that you’re evolving to meet their needs.”

**Section 3: Positioning for Different Segments (1 minute 20 seconds)**

“It’s also important to recognize that different segments of your ICP may value different things. Tailoring your value proposition to specific industries or verticals can make your offering more compelling.

For instance, in SaaS, you might focus on scalability, while in healthcare, you’d emphasize compliance and security. Understanding the nuances of each segment ensures that your offering hits the right pain points.”

*Example:*

“For a SaaS company, you could say, ‘Our platform scales with your business, allowing you to grow without switching tools.’ For healthcare companies, you’d position your product by saying, ‘Our software meets HIPAA compliance standards, ensuring patient data is secure.’ Each message addresses the key concerns of the industry, making it more likely to resonate.”

**Section 4: Scaling Your Value Proposition (1 minute 10 seconds)**

“Once you’ve tested your value proposition on a smaller batch of prospects and refined it based on feedback, it’s time to scale. This means applying your personalized messaging to larger lists of 1000+ leads.

However, even at scale, it’s important to retain a personalized touch where possible. Use segmentation and automation tools to ensure that your prospects still receive messaging that speaks to their specific needs.”

*Example:*

“Let’s say you’ve validated your offering with 200 prospects and fine-tuned your value proposition. Now, when you scale to 1000 leads, you can use email automation to send targeted messages based on industry or job title. By keeping your messaging relevant, you can maintain a high level of engagement even at scale.”

**Conclusion (40 seconds):**

“Personalizing and refining your offering is an ongoing process. By aligning your value proposition with the client journey, responding to feedback, and tailoring your messaging for different segments, you’ll create an offering that resonates on a deeper level. And as you scale, automation and segmentation will help you maintain that personalized touch.

In the next module, we’ll dive into prospecting techniques, including how to build a scalable list of leads and the tools you’ll need to automate your outreach. See you there!”

# Example

**Script for Building an Offering**

**Step 1: Identify the ICP’s Core Problem**

•	**Question to Ask Yourself**:

“What is the biggest pain point or challenge my ICP is trying to solve?”

•	**Example**:

“For marketing teams, the core problem might be measuring ROI accurately. For HR teams, it could be improving employee engagement.”

•	**Fill in the Blank**:

“My ICP’s core problem is ________. This problem is important to them because ________.”

**Step 2: Define the Job to Be Done (JTBD)**

•	**Question to Ask Yourself**:

“What is the job my product is being ‘hired’ to do?”

•	**Example**:

“A project management tool might be hired to improve team collaboration and hit deadlines faster.”

•	**Fill in the Blank**:

“My product is hired to help ________ by ________.”

**Step 3: List the Features of Your Product**

•	**Question to Ask Yourself**:

“What are the key features of my product or service?”

•	**Example**:

“Automation, customizable templates, integration with other tools, analytics dashboard.”

•	**Fill in the Blank**:

“My product has the following features: ________, ________, and ________.”

**Step 4: Translate Features into Benefits**

•	**Question to Ask Yourself**:

“How do these features translate into real-world benefits for my ICP?”

•	**Example**:

“Customizable templates → Saves time on designing campaigns. Analytics dashboard → Provides actionable insights for optimizing marketing efforts.”

•	**Fill in the Blank**:

“These features will help my ICP by ________, ________, and ________.”

**Step 5: Craft the Value Proposition**

•	**Question to Ask Yourself**:

“How do I communicate the benefits of my product in a way that resonates with my ICP?”

•	**Example**:

“Our platform saves your marketing team 10 hours per week by automating reporting and delivering actionable insights, allowing them to focus on strategy.”

•	**Fill in the Blank**:

“My value proposition is: ‘We help ________ by ________, so you can ________.’”

**Step 6: Back It Up with Proof**

•	**Question to Ask Yourself**:

“What results or testimonials can I use to prove that my product delivers on its promise?”

•	**Example**:

“We helped XYZ company reduce campaign management time by 30%.”

•	**Fill in the Blank**:

“Our solution has helped ________ achieve ________, resulting in ________.”

**Step 7: Adjust the Message for Different Stages of the Journey**

•	**Awareness Stage**:

Focus on education: “Did you know that companies spend X hours managing manual reporting every month? Our tool automates that process, giving you that time back.”

•	**Consideration Stage**:

Focus on use cases and comparisons: “Here’s how our tool helped a mid-sized tech company reduce their reporting time by 50%.”

•	**Decision Stage**:

Focus on ROI and specific benefits: “Get started today and reduce your reporting time by 10 hours per week, saving you over $X per year in operational costs.”

**Example of a Completed Script**

**Step 1: Core Problem**

“My ICP’s core problem is accurately tracking marketing ROI. This problem is important because without precise data, they can’t optimize their campaigns or justify their budget.”

**Step 2: Job to Be Done**

“My product is hired to help marketing teams measure the ROI of their campaigns by providing detailed performance data and insights.”

**Step 3: Product Features**

“My product has the following features: automated campaign tracking, a customizable analytics dashboard, and CRM integration.”

**Step 4: Features to Benefits**

“These features will help my ICP by automating the tracking process, reducing manual effort, and providing real-time insights to optimize campaigns.”

**Step 5: Value Proposition**

“We help marketing teams track their campaign performance effortlessly by automating reporting and delivering actionable insights, so you can optimize your marketing strategy and prove ROI.”

**Step 6: Proof**

“Our tool has helped companies like XYZ reduce manual reporting time by 50%, improving their campaign ROI by 20% within six months.”

**Step 7: Journey Adjustment**

•	**Awareness Stage**: “Did you know that many companies spend hours manually tracking their marketing campaigns? We automate this process for you.”

•	**Consideration Stage**: “Here’s a case study showing how we helped a company like yours improve campaign performance and save time.”

•	**Decision Stage**: “Get started today with a free trial and see how much time and money you can save.”

**How to Use This Script**

1.	**Brainstorm and Research**: Use this script as a guide to think critically about your ICP’s pain points, the job your product does, and how to align your offering with their needs.

2.	**Tailor to Your Industry**: Adjust the script for different market segments or stages of the client journey.

3.	**Test and Iterate**: Once you’ve written your value proposition, test it with a small group of prospects. Refine based on the feedback you receive.
