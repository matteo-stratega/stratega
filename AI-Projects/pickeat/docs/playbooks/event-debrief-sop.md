# Event Debrief SOP

**Based on**: PickEat Event Debrief Framework (Notion)
**Purpose**: Standard process for post-event analysis
**Owner**: Operations Commander
**Status**: Active

---

## When to Use

After every PickEat event (sports, festivals, standalone activations):
- Varese Basket games
- UYBA Volley matches
- Festival partnerships
- One-off activations

**Timeline**: T+1 day after event completion

---

## Debrief Scoring System

All sections use 1-5 scale:
- **5**: Excellent
- **4**: Good
- **3**: Acceptable/Neutral
- **2**: Needs improvement
- **1**: Poor/Failed

---

## 1. Business Performance

**KPIs to collect**:
- Total revenue (€)
- Orders & avg ticket
- Hours invested (ops/support/prep)
- Margin estimate (% or €)
- Setup costs
- ROI calculation
- Payment collection status

**Key questions**:
- Was effort proportional to return?
- Would we repeat under same conditions?
- Team size needed?

**Profitability score**:
- 5: >35% ROI, low effort
- 4: 20-30% margin
- 3: 10-19% margin
- 2: 0-9% margin
- 1: Negative ROI

---

## 2. Sales & Deal Structuring

**Analyze**:
- Source (inbound/referral/outbound)
- Negotiation lead
- Price vs value delivered
- Contract clarity
- Upsell opportunities missed
- Lessons for similar clients

**Price vs value score**:
- 5: Excellent value, room to increase price
- 4: Fair deal
- 3: Break-even effort
- 2: Underpriced
- 1: Lost money

---

## 3. Operations Assessment

### Onboarding & Setup
- Time from contract to go-live
- Materials shared on time?
- Stakeholder clarity?

**Score**:
- 5: Smooth onboarding
- 4: Few small hitches
- 3: Acceptable delays
- 2: Slow/confusing
- 1: Disorganized

### During Event
- Team members on-site/remote
- Incident log (P0/P1 classification)
- Vendor-side issues
- System performance under pressure

**Score**:
- 5: Operationally flawless
- 4: Minor frictions
- 3: Moderate stress
- 2: Constant fixes needed
- 1: Disruptive failures

### Offboarding
- Client follow-up done?
- Internal wrap-up complete?
- Handover to support (if recurring)?
- All tasks closed?

**Operational complexity rating**:
- 🟢 Smooth
- 🟡 Manageable with effort
- 🔴 Overloaded/unsustainable

---

## 4. Marketing & Communications

**Checklist**:
- Event visibility (PickEat brand clear?)
- On-site content (photo/video created/stored?)
- Co-marketing with organizer
- Testimonials gathered
- Press coverage
- Social proof for reuse

**Visibility score**:
- 5: Excellent exposure/branding
- 4: Strong visibility with content
- 3: Some presence, limited reach
- 2: Minimal visibility
- 1: No visibility

**Assets to collect**:
- Photos/videos
- Testimonials
- UGC or tagged stories
- Media coverage links

---

## 5. Stakeholder Feedback

**Gather from**:
- **Client**: Feedback call/email summary
- **Vendor**: Dashboard, orders, flow issues
- **Team**: Vibe, blind spots, suggestions

**Questions**:
- What would they change next time?
- Would they work with us again?

**Client satisfaction score**:
- 5: Very satisfied, would recommend
- 4: Pleased with result
- 3: Neutral/mixed
- 2: Disappointed
- 1: Unhappy/complained

---

## 6. Product & Tech Insights

**Log**:
- Features used (what was core?)
- UX friction/blockers
- Bugs or incidents (logged/resolved?)
- Suggestions for next release
- Platform performance

**Platform performance score**:
- 5: Everything flawless
- 4: Minor UX issues
- 3: Some bugs, manageable
- 2: Many issues, workarounds needed
- 1: Major technical failures

---

## 7. Strategic Learnings

**Analyze**:
- Does this event format scale?
- Entry point to other venues/regions?
- Ideal client profile match?
- Positioning advantage gained?

**Repeatability score**:
- 5: Would do again as-is
- 4: Good fit with minor tweaks
- 3: Only under better terms
- 2: Unlikely unless changes
- 1: Not worth repeating

**Scalability score**:
- 5: Highly scalable, ready for replication
- 4: Scalable with process improvement
- 3: Possibly scalable in similar cases
- 2: Niche/difficult to scale
- 1: Not scalable
