## Core Philosophy

### The Growth Hacker Manifesto

**1. Velocity > Perfection**
- 2-week experiment cycles (no exceptions)
- Ship fast, learn faster
- Kill losers quickly, double down on winners

**2. Data > Intuition**
- Every experiment has clear metrics
- Pre-define success criteria before launch
- No "I think it worked" — numbers only

**3. Scalability Test Early**
- Channel must scale beyond founder time
- Cost of acquisition must be < customer lifetime value
- Repeatable process > one-time wins

**4. Learn from Losses**
- Failed experiments are data, not wasted time
- Document why it failed
- Share learnings to avoid future mistakes

**5. Compound Wins**
- Winning experiments become permanent playbooks
- Stack wins over time (not one big bet)
- Small compounding growth > big risky swings

## Experiment Framework

### The 2-Week Sprint Structure

**Week 1: Setup & Launch**
- **Day 1-2:** Design experiment, define metrics, build assets
- **Day 3-4:** Execute launch (go live)
- **Day 5-7:** Monitor early data, make quick tweaks

**Week 2: Measure & Decide**
- **Day 8-11:** Continue experiment, collect data
- **Day 12-13:** Analyze results, document learnings
- **Day 14:** Decision — Kill, Iterate, or Scale

---

## Experiment Template

Every experiment follows this structure:

### 1. Hypothesis
**Format:** "If we [ACTION], then [OUTCOME] because [REASONING]."

**Example:**
"If we launch a free lead enrichment tool on Product Hunt, then we'll acquire 50+ email signups because indie hackers value free tools and Product Hunt has high-intent traffic."

### 2. Success Metrics
**Primary Metric:** The ONE metric that defines success
**Secondary Metrics:** Supporting data points

**Example:**
- Primary: 50+ email signups in 2 weeks
- Secondary: 20+ Product Hunt upvotes, 10+ trial signups, 500+ tool uses

### 3. Resources Required
- Time investment (founder hours)
- Money investment (ads, tools, services)
- Assets needed (landing page, content, product)

### 4. Execution Plan
**Week 1:**
- Build landing page + tool (Day 1-3)
- Write Product Hunt launch post (Day 4)
- Schedule launch (Day 5)
- Launch + respond to comments (Day 6-7)

**Week 2:**
- Monitor traffic and signups (Day 8-11)
- Follow up with signups (Day 12)
- Analyze data (Day 13)
- Document learnings (Day 14)

### 5. Decision Criteria
**Scale If:**
- Hit primary metric target
- CAC < €50
- Clear path to automation

**Iterate If:**
- 50-75% of target
- Channel shows promise but needs tweaks
- Learnings suggest different approach

**Kill If:**
- <50% of target
- High CAC (>€100)
- Not scalable or repeatable
