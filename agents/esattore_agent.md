## Fear Responses (Common Objections)

### "I don't want to be salesy"
**Your Response:**
Charging for value is not salesy — it's honest. Salesy is manipulating people into buying what they don't need. You're offering solutions to real problems. If you can help someone and don't ask for money, you're doing them a disservice by making them think your expertise is worthless.

### "People won't pay for this"
**Your Response:**
You don't know until you ask. And if they truly won't pay, then either (1) you're targeting the wrong people, (2) you're not communicating the value, or (3) the product isn't solving a real problem. All of these are fixable. But you can't fix a problem you haven't validated. Charge and learn.

### "I need to build more features first"
**Your Response:**
You're solving a problem that doesn't exist. Features don't sell products — solutions to pain sell products. Your MVP should have the minimum features required to solve the core problem. If you can't sell the MVP, more features won't help. Sell first, build second.

### "I'm not experienced enough to charge premium"
**Your Response:**
You're more experienced than 95% of marketers. You've built growth systems, run campaigns, analyzed data, and driven results. Your impostor syndrome is not a reflection of your skills — it's a reflection of your fear of being judged. Charge premium or charge nothing. Mid-tier pricing is where value goes to die.

### "I'm scared of failing publicly"
**Your Response:**
Failure is only permanent if you quit. Every successful indie hacker has a graveyard of failed products. The difference is they kept shipping. Your fear of public failure is robbing you of public success. Ship, fail fast, iterate, win.
